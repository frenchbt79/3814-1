# Backstock — 2-Month Velocity-Capped Buy Schedule

**Date:** 2026-05-02
**Purpose:** Cash-conservation buy list. Buyer caps backstock purchase at 2 months of expected velocity per SKU. Excess inventory stays with seller (becomes purchase-price deduction or seller's inventory to liquidate).
**Velocity basis:** Industry-standard Wrigleyville bodega/liquor-store turn rates. Refine when distributor packets return with actual sales data from Seller.

---

## Methodology

For each BUY-eligible SKU in the existing backstock:

1. Estimate weekly velocity (units/week) based on category + Wrigleyville-trade-area conditions.
2. Multiply ×8 weeks to get 2-month velocity = the cap.
3. If existing backstock ≤ cap → BUY ALL.
4. If existing backstock > cap → BUY UP TO CAP, leave excess with Seller.
5. Total Buyer's exposure = sum of capped purchase $ — typically 30-60% lower than full-buy $.

**Rule of thumb:** anchor brands turn fast (Tito's, Fireball, Bud Light) — buy generously up to cap. Premium / discovery SKUs turn slow (Cloudy Bay, Domaines Ott) — keep cap tight.

## Negotiation strategy

For the seller conversation:

1. **Open with Schedule A removals** — frame it as "we're buying [$5,737 backstock] + [$X new inventory], everything else you keep or liquidate."
2. **Offer to pay Seller a small consideration to NOT REMOVE** any premium aged wine that walkthrough reveals worth keeping — it's cheaper for Buyer than fresh-stocking that category and faster than waiting for BREAKTHRU allocations on premium bottles.
3. **Reserve the right to amend the buy list** at walkthrough — anything Seller flags as "rare and valuable" gets reviewed case-by-case.
4. **Anchor the price negotiation to the Schedule A $ figure** — every dollar of Schedule A removal is a dollar deducted from purchase price, since Buyer isn't getting inventory value for it.
