## Read this first

The careful re-audit surfaced **~40 discrepancies across the 18 cooler doors with photos** (D1-D6, D11-D22). They sort into 4 categories:

1. **Architecture bugs** — wrong fixture/category mapping at the wall level (e.g. D11/D12 are beer, not functional bev / fresh food). These cascade to floor plan, master sheet, distributor packets.
2. **AFTER-state SKU leakage in BEFORE arrays** — universal across the doors. Comes from `status:'new'` tiles being authored inline in the same shelf data structure as `keep`/`remove` tiles. The renderer filters them out of the BEFORE pane correctly, BUT the flat `SKUDB` array (lines 700-1000) and several other rollups don't filter — so the leakage shows up in distributor packets, master inventory rollups, and the floor plan SVG color coding.
3. **Cosmetic per-shelf fixes** — wrong facing counts, missing SKUs, phantom SKUs, misidentifications.
4. **Photo-coverage gaps** — D5/D6 boundary unclear (re-walk needed); D11 photo and D11.jpg have D11=beer-12pk but the AFTER plan calls D11 functional bev — this is by design (repositioning thesis); just need explicit BEFORE/AFTER separation.

I need a **go/no-go per category** before I write any Python scripts. Below is each finding ranked by severity. Mark each row "GO", "PARK", or "RE-SHOOT" and we're off.
